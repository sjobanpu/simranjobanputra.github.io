BioLogic

Timeline: One Semester (Fall 2018)

My Role: Strategy

Target User: Patients at UPMC Hospitals

Key Methods: Expert Interviewing, Contextual Inquiry, Wireframing, Process

BioLogic is a responsive fashion material, commonly known as a “Second Skin”. The synthetic bio-skin reacts to body heat and sweat,
causing flaps around heat zones to open, enabling sweat to evaporate and cool down the body through an organic material flux. In
collaboration with New Balance, bioLogic is bringing what once may have lived in the realm of fantasies into the world of sportswear.
The project was brought to Carnegie Mellon through Lining Yao, a past PhD student at the MIT Media Lab that was in charge of
concept creation, interaction design, and fabrication.

The Making of BioLogic

Application at UPMC Hospitals

My contribution to the project was creating a strategy pitch on how BioLogic could be used in order to more efficiently recognize
the symptoms of sick patients, such as those increasingly at risk of having a fever or sweating from increased palpitations.
Due to upcoming presentations for implementation and funding, I'm unable to disclose further details of the project currently.
