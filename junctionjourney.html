The Junction Journey

Click to View Prototype




Timeline: Two Weeks

My Role: Rapid Prototyping, Pitching/Strategy, Process Book

Target User: Children at local schools faculty

Key Methods: Affinity Diagramming, Survey Design, Hueristic Evaluation, Interviewing, Storyboarding, Stakeholder Mapping

Our client is Construction Junction and they are well known in Pittsburgh for the reused & recycled goods they sell. However, they also want to raise
awareness of their events that focus on helping the local community better understand the environment and recycling. Junction Journey just does that
by subtly marketing Construction Junction through an interactive game for students. The game can be something that can be played at school for its
educational purpose and can be brought back home to continue gaming and sharing the word with parents, in turn targeting two major user groups.
Through targeting children and classrooms, we are using the ultime technique of Word of Mouth and reaching more users than just social media
marketing. Plus, it's super fun for the kids with its animations and VR settings, while they learn to become better citizens for the future through gaming.

Stakeholders

We conducted basic research on their social media and realized Construction had employed everything they could but wasn't getting much awareness
or circulation. Thus, we decided to take a look at the stakeholders to see if there was a group we could target to improve our marketing efforts.

It was evident from our analysis, that Pittsburgh had a large educational community, which is why we decided to move towards the idea of an educational
app. We then brainstormed the best way to reach students, teachers, colleges, elementary schools, etc. We found it best to target school-age children
(middle school or below) since they regularly conversate with their parents (targetting two groups).

Gamifying the Experience.

Making the educational tool more fun and competitive would increase student usage. Here are some features we added.



We created three different types of interactions which are the three rows above. Each of the rows has built in microinteractions, such as toggling features.
The first row is focused on personal achievement, where we allow the student to win badges based on challenges they achieve. The center panel is an
animated milestone anytime they achieve thier target.The second row is for competition, where students can compete with others at their school,
classrooms can compete with students at other schools, and there are monthly challenges to excite students about becoming more environmentally
friendly. Finally, the third row is about tying in Construction Junction's outlook. By using VR interactions, we allow students to figure out what can be
recyled/reused in their houses and can be brought to the warehouse to be repurposed, sort of bringing the mobile app full circle.



This is a plan for how we would introduce our interactive mobile app in classrooms across Pittsburgh.
