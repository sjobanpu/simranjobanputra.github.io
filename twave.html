tWave

Timeline: Three Weeks

My Role: Data Modeling, Pitch, Strategy

Target User: Anyone that travels or uses public transportation

Key Methods: Sketch, Adobe After Effects, Storyboard, Scenarios

Quickly extracting departure and arrival information while you travel is extremely important. It's especially important when you are
traveling via different modes of transporation or have connections. Our stakeholders asked us the question of streamlining travel
between flight, ferry, and train schedules in order to make traveling more hassle free. My team and I solved this issue through creating
Twave, a data driven display for transportation information. Our key goal was to create more intuitive travel screens that could be more
accessible to large audiences, accomplish the specific requirements of our stakeholders, and mitigate the risks associated with
misconception and misinterpretation during travel.

Stakeholders & Transportation Goals

There were three main types of stakeholders we dealt with: the traveling employee, the retired grandfather visiting his grandchildren
and one of the managers of the transportation systems that really wants customers to use the PTF pass (Plane, Train, Ferry) in order
to make the most use of transportation services.



Inspiration & Research

Our team created an inspiration board in order to look at what kinds of colors and typefaces would look best. Additionally, we also took into account
important situational factors - such as various heights of users and display screen location within the Transportation Hub. The wordle describes
some of the issues we discussed.









Key Considerations

Layout

How easily a user might read the board’s contents impacts how quickly they can use the information. In the context of a transportation hub, time is critical
and a few considerations include: attention to logo styling and placement, use of color to indicate status changes (delays, on-time, etc.), and optimizing
layout/visual language for the given screen size, with the potential to display on multiple screen sizes (large screen, desktop, mobile).

Placement

Where the board is located within the airport, station, or ferry terminal plays a key role in the experience of the user. We need to ensure that multiple
users can access the information at the same time and the board is easy to discover/is in an ideal location.

User Context

Within the context of a transportation hub, users will bring additional complexity to an interaction with the display board. A successful design will anticipate
that users will be in a rush and will need information quickly, users might be first-timers and not experts with the interface, and displays will be accessed
in multiple lighting, crowd, and ambient conditions by users of various heights, visual abilities, cultures, backgrounds, and language fluency levels.



Iteration

We iterated on color, typeface, and the display itself - inspired by a visual layout used in the Vienna International Airport. On our iteration we received that
there was far too much text and the legends were difficult to read.
